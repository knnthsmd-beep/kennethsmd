Batangas Lomi Recipe

Ingredients

  • 500g fresh miki noodles (or thick egg noodles)
  • 300g pork, thinly sliced (or chicken/beef)
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup kikiam or fish balls (optional)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cups chicken or pork broth
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch slurry (cornstarch mixed with water)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for sautéing
  • Optional toppings: crushed chicharon, green onions, calamansi

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant and translucent, about 2-3 minutes.
  2. Add the sliced pork (or your choice of meat) and cook until browned, about 5 minutes. If using shrimp, add them now and cook for 2 minutes until pink.
  3. Pour in the broth and bring to a boil. Add soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes to develop flavors.
  4. Add carrots, cabbage, and kikiam/fish balls. Cook for another 5 minutes until vegetables are tender.
  5. Stir in the miki noodles and cook for 3-5 minutes until softened. If the noodles are dry, blanch them separately first.
  6. Gradually add the cornstarch slurry while stirring continuously to thicken the broth. Simmer until it reaches a thick, gravy-like consistency, about 2-3 minutes.
  7. Turn off the heat and slowly drizzle in the beaten eggs while stirring to create egg ribbons.
  8. Serve hot in bowls, topped with crushed chicharon, chopped green onions, and a squeeze of calamansi for extra flavor.
